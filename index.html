The Problem

The metro ride is the easy part.
It's the 18 minutes after that breaks the commute.

India's metros are expanding fast, but ridership consistently underperforms. The reason isn't trunk-route quality — it's the unpredictable gap between station and destination.

🚗

Autos Are Unreliable

Inconsistent availability, fare volatility, and "short ride nahi" refusals make autos an unpredictable last-mile option every single morning.

💰

Ride-Hailing Is Expensive

Surge pricing on bike taxis and cabs can hit ₹94+ for a 2 km ride. Commuters are pushed back to private vehicles, undermining transit investment.

🤔

No Single Source of Truth

Today's workaround is a stack of Google Maps + WhatsApp groups + local knowledge. Functional, but fragile and exhausting to repeat daily.

The Solution

One app. Station to office. Sorted.

FlowCity does three jobs extremely well for metro-linked office commuters.

Smart Commute Cards

Save your route once. Every morning, see today's best low-cost route, most reliable option, and a backup — no searching required. The card is your default screen.

Confidence-Rated Options

Every recommendation carries a reliability score with plain-language reasoning: "shared autos delayed" or "walking faster than waiting." Three options, clear tradeoffs, no guesswork.

Station Intelligence

Best exit gates, wait times by mode, walking vs. ride comparisons, verified pickup points, and rainy-day adjustments — operational intelligence that generic maps don't surface.

Budget Bands

Options in three honest bands: under ₹30, ₹30–60, and ₹60+ fastest. No rigid fare promises that break trust. Set your preferred band; the app optimises within it.

Offline-Ready

Station exits, connector points, walking maps, and fallback routes work even when you're underground or in a congested area with poor network.

Exception Handling

Missed your connector? One-tap alternate pickup points, contextual nudges like "walk 6 min to save 12 min waiting," and clear fallback guidance.

Business Model

Employer-side monetisation first

Employers bear the cost of late arrivals, commute-driven attrition, and underutilised office space. They pay before consumers do.

Phase 1 — Months 1–6

Employer Partnerships & Connector Placement

B2B2C commute programs for major tech parks. Paid placement for verified shuttle and connector services, clearly labelled and trust-preserving.

Phase 2 — Months 6–12

Referral Fees & Corporate Dashboards

Referral commissions from connector providers. Corporate commute dashboards for HR and facilities teams, powered by aggregated commuter data.

Phase 3 — Year 2+

Bundled Passes & Integrated Booking

Corridor-level demand forecasting, bundled commute passes, and integrated booking commissions as FlowCity becomes the corridor infrastructure layer.
